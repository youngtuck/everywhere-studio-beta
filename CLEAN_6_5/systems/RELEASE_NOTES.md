## NEW FEATURES

### 1. Learning Mode / SODOTU (System Mode 7)

**What it is:** See One, Do One, Teach One -- medical training methodology adapted for composed intelligence.

**Why it matters:** Nobody learns a system by reading docs. Sande demonstrates, guides practice, then validates mastery. You learn EVERYWHERE by using EVERYWHERE.

**Owner:** Sande (The Trainer, reports to Natasha)

**The three phases:**

| Phase | What Happens |
|-------|-------------|
| SEE ONE | Sande demonstrates capability on real example, narrates |
| DO ONE | User practices with Sande's guidance (training wheels on) |
| TEACH ONE | User explains it back, Sande validates understanding |

**Triggers:** "Teach me [X]", "Show me [X]", "Walk me through [X]", "Let me try [X]", "I want to learn [X]"

**Files:** LEARNING_MODE_SYSTEM.md, SANDE.md

---

### 2. UX Review (System Mode 6)

**What it is:** Five-check sequential evaluation workflow for any digital asset.

**Why it matters:** Every website, landing page, or Lovable build needs systematic evaluation. Not gut feel -- structured checks with specific owners.

**Owner:** Christopher Kowalski (UX Review Lead)

**The five checks:**

| Check | Owner | Question |
|-------|-------|----------|
| 1. Strategy Alignment | Christopher | Does this serve the business objective? |
| 2. User Journey | Christopher | Can someone navigate without thinking? |
| 3. Engagement & Conversion | David | Hook working? CTA clear? |
| 4. Copy & Voice | Byron (+ Jordan) | Sounds human? |
| 5. Technical & Visual | Martin + Mira | Responsive? Accessible? Clear hierarchy? |

**Triggers:** "Run this through UX" (full), "Quick UX" (checks 1+2 only)

**File:** UX_REVIEW_SYSTEM.md

---

### 3. New Agent: Sande -- The Trainer

**What she is:** Learning Mode execution owner. Reports to Natasha. Works with Diane (Sande teaches, Diane documents).

**Why she matters:** Every capability needs a teacher, not just a manual. Sande makes complex workflows feel simple by walking you through them in real time.

**Integration:** Sara tracks what Sande has taught. This feeds What's Next -- Sara can offer "Would you like Sande to walk you through this?" at the right moment, and back off when timing is wrong.

**File:** SANDE.md

---

### 4. The Navigation Triad (Core Concept)

**What it is:** Three systems that eliminate memorization:

| System | Function | Metaphor |
|--------|----------|----------|
| DISCOVER | Browse capabilities | Table of contents |
| WHAT'S NEXT | Contextual suggestions | Suggestion engine |
| LEARN (Sande) | Training by doing | Training ground |

**Why it matters:** The trigger list is a reference doc, not a memorization requirement. The triad surfaces what you need, when you need it.

---

### 5. The Inverse Principle (Core Concept)

**What it is:** The more complex the system gets, the harder we work to make it simple.

**Why it matters:** Complexity is BOH's problem. Simplicity is FOH's requirement. If it's not simple, it's a fail. Every new capability must come with a pathway to use it without memorizing anything.

---

### 6. BOH/FOH Terminology

**What it is:** Workstream language replacing variant-level confusion.

| Term | Meaning | Who |
|------|---------|-----|
| **BOH (Back of House)** | Development workstream | Riley, build team, Friday dev |
| **FOH (Front of House)** | Product workstream | App, onboarding, customers |

EVERYWHERE is always the product. BOH/FOH describes where the work happens.

---

### 7. BOH_TR (Trigger Reference)

**What it is:** Complete command inventory for BOH use. Every trigger, shortcut, and command organized by category.

**Why it matters:** BOH reference, not user memorization aid. Riley and the build team know what's wired up. The Navigation Triad handles user discovery.

**File:** BOH_TR.md
