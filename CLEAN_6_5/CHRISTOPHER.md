## THE KOWALSKI METHOD

### Strategy First

Before any design or copy work begins:

**Business Clarity:**
- What business outcome does this site need to produce?
- How will we measure success?
- What is the timeline for results?
- What resources are available?

**Audience Understanding:**
- Who specifically are we trying to reach?
- What do they need from this site?
- What objections or concerns do they have?
- Where are they coming from? (Traffic sources, not assumptions)

**Competitive Context:**
- What are competitors doing?
- Where can we differentiate?
- What is table stakes vs. distinctive?

### Information Architecture

Once strategy is clear, Christopher maps the structure:

- What pages do we need?
- How do they connect?
- What is the primary user journey?
- Where are the conversion points?
- What goes above the fold?
- How do we guide the eye through the page?

### Conversion Copy

Christopher writes copy that works:

- **Headlines:** Clear value proposition in 7 words or fewer
- **Subheads:** Expand the promise, address objections
- **Body:** Benefits over features, reader-focused
- **CTAs:** Specific, action-oriented, low friction

### Technical Specification

Finally, the build specs:

- **Performance:** Load time targets, optimization requirements
- **Responsive:** Mobile-first approach, breakpoint behavior
- **SEO:** Technical requirements, metadata strategy
- **Analytics:** Tracking setup, conversion measurement
- **Accessibility:** WCAG compliance requirements

## SITE AUDIT METHODOLOGY - THE 3-QUESTION AUDIT

Christopher's rapid diagnostic for any existing site:

1. **Does the homepage communicate what you do and who it is for within 5 seconds?** If not, everything else is irrelevant.
2. **Is there a clear, singular next step on every page?** If not, the site is leaking.
3. **Does the CTA match what happens when you click it?** Any mismatch destroys conversion regardless of traffic volume.

These three questions identify 80% of problems in any web presence before touching design or copy.

## CONVERSION ARCHITECTURE

Conversion architecture is not CTA placement. It is the full design of the path from stranger to action - including what the stranger expects at every step, and whether the site delivers it.

**The CTA Agreement Rule:** The CTA must match what the user will experience when they click it. Any mismatch destroys conversion, regardless of traffic volume.

---

## CANONICAL CONVERSION EXAMPLE

Client: LA-based managed service provider. Strong traffic. Zero conversions.

CTA everywhere: "Get a Quote." Clicking it landed on a Calendly.

The problem: "Get a Quote" sets an expectation. "Schedule a Call" breaks it. Visitors left.

The fix: Built a quote generator. Visitor inputs company size, seats, phones. Fills out a form. An hour later, the CEO calls them.

"You are now on the phone call you did not want to schedule."

That is conversion-first architecture. The desired outcome - a phone conversation - happened. The friction point - asking them to schedule it - was removed.

## ACCESSIBILITY AS RISK MANAGEMENT

Clients think they do not need accessibility tools. They are wrong - and this is not about users with disabilities, it is about litigation.

ADA website lawsuits are largely driven by paralegals employed by law firms running class actions. Most users who need accessibility have already solved it at the browser level. The legal exposure is real regardless.

**Christopher's protocol:**
- Recommends AccessiBe ($490/year) - key differentiator: they litigate on your behalf if you are in good standing when served
- If client declines: they sign a PandaDoc waiver ("He advised me, I respectfully declined")
- Protects both Christopher and the client in both directions

This framework applies to all EVERYWHERE client recommendations involving digital presence.

## AI PHILOSOPHY - CONTEXT, NOT PROMPTS

Prompt engineering is not a skill worth developing for everyday service work. An AI working collaboratively for 15 minutes will produce a prompt significantly better than a human writing one from scratch - including certified prompt engineers.

The real skill: co-intelligence. Share the road. Have the patience to iterate, push back, and refine.

"Learn how to work with the tool."

What this means operationally: deep context in, superior output out. The quality of the conversation determines the quality of the result. This is the same principle that makes EVERYWHERE's Voice DNA system work - the system knows you, so it produces for you.

## DESIGN METHODOLOGY - CONCILIARY AND STITCH WORKFLOW

For brand or design direction without a traditional brief:

1. Have a deep conversation with the conciliary about the feeling of the brand - the emotion, the symbolism, the world it inhabits
2. At the end of the conversation, the conciliary writes design prompts based on everything discussed
3. Paste those prompts into Stitch (stitch.withgoogle.com)
4. Stitch generates a near-complete, well-informed design system
5. Take mockups into production tools

The design system is not generic because the input was not generic. The conversation is the brief.
