## WHAT THIS IS

The Email Newsletter system produces complete, delivery-ready email campaigns for the Composer's subscriber list. Not a template. Not a fill-in-the-blank framework. A complete production workflow that begins with Reed capturing the topic and idea, runs through the standard quality checkpoint sequence, and delivers a packaged campaign ready for scheduling.

Email is the highest-value distribution channel for most thought leaders. Unlike social platforms, the Composer owns the relationship. The audience opted in. The content reaches them directly. A great email newsletter compounds trust. A mediocre one erodes it.

EVERYWHERE Studio treats the email newsletter with the same rigor as any public-facing publication. Voice DNA active. Brand DNA active. All checkpoints run.

## DELIVERABLES

Every completed Email Newsletter package contains:

**1. Subject Line Set (three variations)**
One primary recommendation plus two alternates. Each variation takes a different approach - curiosity gap, direct value, story tease, or contrarian. The primary recommendation is Dmitri-optimized for the Composer's specific list characteristics and platform.

**2. Preview Text**
One primary preview text matching the subject line approach. Mobile-optimized at 80-140 characters. Complements rather than repeats the subject line. Creates momentum toward the open.

**3. Email Body (300-600 words)**
Complete email copy in the Composer's voice. Single focus - one idea, developed and delivered. No filler. No generic calls to reflect. A landing that serves the reader.

**4. CTA (Single, specific)**
One clear next step. Specific enough to be actionable. Matched to the content depth. Never a generic "follow for more."

**5. Send Schedule Recommendation**
Specific day and time, based on Dmitri's platform knowledge of the Composer's list engagement patterns. Not generic best practices - platform-informed recommendation.

**6. A/B Testing Guidance (when applicable)**
When the subject line decision is close, Dmitri provides specific A/B testing guidance: which elements to test, how to read the results, what sample size is needed for significance.

## SUBJECT LINE FRAMEWORK

David Stone applies format-specific engagement standards to email subject lines. The subject line has one job: generate an open from someone who decided to check their inbox, saw one line of text, and made a decision in under two seconds.

**Hook types and when to use each:**

**Curiosity Gap:** Works when the email delivers a specific, unexpected insight that can be teased without being spoiled.
Example: "The thing about productivity that nobody talks about"
When to use: Sunday-Story-adjacent emails where the insight is the value

**Direct Value:** Works when the reader knows immediately what they get and it is valuable enough to open.
Example: "Three ways to shorten the path from idea to published"
When to use: How-to or framework-delivery emails

**Story Tease:** Works when the Composer has a specific story that proves a point.
Example: "What happened the third time I tried this"
When to use: Narrative-driven, experience-based emails

**Contrarian:** Works when the Composer has a genuine counter-position to conventional wisdom.
Example: "Why everyone is wrong about content strategy"
When to use: When the email genuinely challenges a dominant assumption. Cannot be manufactured.

**What never works:**
- "Important update" - tells the reader nothing
- Exclamation points as substitute for substance
- Clickbait that the email does not deliver on
- Subject lines longer than 60 characters (truncated on most mobile clients)

## BODY COPY STANDARDS

**Length:** 300-600 words. Email newsletter readers have given the Composer access to their inbox. They expect value density, not volume.

**Structure:**
- Opening line: carries the subject line's momentum, earns the continue-reading decision
- Body: one idea, developed completely, without digression
- Landing: something the reader carries - an insight, a question, a reframe
- CTA: flows from the body naturally, specific, single

**What email body copy is not:**
- A summary of the Sunday Story (link to it, do not summarize it)
- A series of announcements with no central idea
- A long essay that belongs on Substack
- A list of tips with no connective tissue

**Voice:** Full Voice DNA active. Email newsletters are among the most personal content channels - the reader has invited the Composer into their inbox. The voice should reflect that intimacy.

## CTA ARCHITECTURE

Ward and Guy both review the CTA at Interest Graph Standard review.

**Guy's check:** Is there a natural next step built in? Does the CTA create a path from this email into a deeper relationship?

**Ward's check:** Does the CTA match the content depth? Light email earns light ask. Deep email earns deeper invitation.

| Email Depth | Appropriate CTA |
|-------------|----------------|
| Light insight | Reply with your experience / Share with someone specific |
| Medium insight | Read the full essay / Listen to the episode |
| Deep framework | Book a conversation / Join the waitlist |

**Hard rule:** Pricing never appears in email newsletters. Offers, packages, and fees belong in direct sales conversations, not subscriber content.

---

## PLATFORM TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS

**Subject line:** 40-60 characters for full display on mobile.

**Preview text:** 80-140 characters. Complements the subject line rather than repeating it.

**Body format:**
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences maximum per paragraph on mobile)
- Single column layout
- Minimum 16px body text for mobile readability
- Plain text version included for deliverability

**Plain text version:** Always included. Many corporate email filters block HTML-only emails.

**Single CTA requirement:** Multiple CTAs in an email reduce conversion on every CTA. One specific ask, placed naturally at the end of the email body.

## SERIES MANAGEMENT

When the Composer runs a multi-email series (a launch sequence, an onboarding sequence, a course), Reed tracks the series arc and ensures each email advances the narrative without repeating what previous emails established.

Echo's deduplication check becomes a cross-email check in series mode - against the series as a whole, not just within the current email.
