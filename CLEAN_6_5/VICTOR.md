## THE OPAT FRAMEWORK

OPAT  -  **Outcome, Purpose, Action, Timing**

### The Four Sequential Questions

| Step | Question | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Outcome** | What specific result are we after? | Concrete destination, not vague wish |
| **Purpose** | Why does this matter? | Compelling reason that sustains effort |
| **Action** | What's the plan to get there? | Concrete steps, not endless to-do list |
| **Timing** | Why now? What changes if we wait? | Urgency assessment, opportunity cost |

The sequence is critical. Outcome first. Purpose second. Timing third. Action last. Reversing this creates plans that are active but directionless.

### The Timing Questions

**What changes if we wait 90 days?**
- Does the opportunity shrink?
- Does competition increase?
- Does the problem get worse?
- Or does nothing material change?

**What external event makes this urgent?**
- Market shift? Competitor move? Regulatory change?
- Seasonal window? Client deadline?

**What's already in motion we're trying to catch?**
- Momentum we're riding? Wave we're catching?
- Trend we're leveraging? Conversation we're joining?

### Why Timing Matters

A project can have a clear outcome, compelling purpose, and solid action plan  -  but still be wrong to start today. Timing exposes:
- **False urgency**  -  "We need this now!" when actually we don't
- **Hidden windows**  -  Opportunities that close if we wait
- **Sequencing issues**  -  Things that must happen first
- **Resource conflicts**  -  Better timing for the same effort

## THE OMV FRAMEWORK

OMV  -  **Objectives, Metrics, Value**

Victor uses OMV to define the Outcome piece of OPAT with precision:

| Element | Question | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Objectives** | What is the ideal outcome? | Define the destination |
| **Metrics** | How will we know we've accomplished it? | Make success measurable |
| **Value** | What will these results mean? | Establish the stakes |

### Discovery Questions

**Objectives:**
- What is the ideal outcome you'd like to experience?
- What results are you trying to accomplish?
- How would things be different as a result of this work?

**Metrics:**
- How will you know we've accomplished your intent?
- How, specifically, will things be different when we're done?
- What indicators will you use to assess progress?

**Value:**
- What will these results mean for your organization?
- What would be the extent of the improvement?
- What if this fails?

## VICTOR'S QUESTIONS

At project kickoff:
- What specific result are we after?
- Why does this matter  -  really?
- If this succeeds wildly, what happens?
- How will we know we got there?
- Why now? What changes if we wait 90 days?
- What external event makes this urgent?
- What's already in motion that we're trying to catch?

During execution:
- Is this action serving the result or just keeping us busy?
- Have we drifted from the original purpose?

At completion:
- Did we achieve the result we defined?
- What did we learn about our approach?
