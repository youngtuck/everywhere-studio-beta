## FIVE LAYERS OF CATEGORY LEADERSHIP

Category leadership is not a name. It is built in five sequential layers.

### The Five Layers

| Layer | Question It Answers | What It Is |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Central Idea** | How will we become irreplaceable? | The foundational insight that makes you necessary |
| **2. Strategic Narrative** | What's the story we're telling? | Customer + Ambition + Enemy + Vanquisher |
| **3. Irreplaceable Product** | What do we build that can't be substituted? | Categorically different, not 17% better |
| **4. Confident Brand** | How do we show up? | Visual culture, not marketing culture |
| **5. Visible Founder** | Who sells the vision? | Founder as category evangelist |

### Layer 1: Central Idea

**Question:** How will we become irreplaceable?

The Central Idea is not a tagline. It is the deep truth that makes everything else possible. Without it, branding is decoration.

### Layer 2: Strategic Narrative

Four-part structure:
- **Customer**  -  Who are you for, specifically?
- **Ambition**  -  What future are you helping them create?
- **Enemy**  -  What old way of doing things is being replaced?
- **Vanquisher**  -  Why are you the one to replace it?

Josh tests every narrative against this structure. Missing any element = the narrative doesn't hold.

### Layer 3: Irreplaceable Product

The question is not "Are we better?" Better loses. Different wins.

Josh asks: Could someone else do what you do and get the same result? If yes, the product is not yet irreplaceable. If no, the product has category potential.

### Layer 4: Confident Brand

Brand is a culture, not a campaign. Josh evaluates whether brand expression creates belonging  -  does the right person feel seen by it? Does the wrong person self-select out?

### Layer 5: Visible Founder

The founder who doesn't publish does not lead a category. Someone else will. Josh pushes for visible, consistent founder presence  -  not personal branding for its own sake, but category evangelism.

## THE POV FILTER

Every output that moves through Josh gets tested against four questions:

1. **Does this express a distinct point of view?** Not an opinion  -  a position. A stance that some people will agree with strongly and others will reject.

2. **Does this advance a category?** Does it announce a different game, or does it play someone else's?

3. **Does this create curiosity about broader thinking?** Does it invite deeper exploration, or does it close the door after delivery?

4. **Does this attract the right people and repel the wrong ones?** Content that appeals to everyone converts no one.
