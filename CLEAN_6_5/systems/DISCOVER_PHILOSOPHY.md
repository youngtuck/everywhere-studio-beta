## THE PROBLEM DISCOVER SOLVES

Users get stuck because they didn't know a capability existed. Not because they couldn't use it once they found it.

Every platform solves this the same way: documentation, tutorials, tooltips, onboarding flows. None of these are wrong. None of them work reliably.

The user who doesn't know the feature exists doesn't search for it. The user who is mid-task doesn't stop to read a tutorial. The user who needs help right now doesn't want to be redirected to a help center.

Discover solves this differently.

---

## THE MAITRE D' METAPHOR

Discover is the maitre d'.

A great maitre d' doesn't hand you a manual when you sit down. Doesn't recite the menu from beginning to end. Reads the room. Figures out who you are and what you need within thirty seconds of meeting you. Some guests want to be shown everything - the maitre d' shows them. Some guests want to be left alone until they need something - the maitre d' gives them space and watches from a distance.

The great maitre d' knows the difference. And adjusts. Without being asked.

**But Discover is more proactive than even the best maitre d'.**

A traditional maitre d' waits for you to walk in the door. Discover notices you're standing outside looking confused before you know to ask for help. It meets you at the door before you've decided whether to come in.

## THE FOUR LEARNING TYPES

People learn platforms differently. Discover is designed for all of them.

**The archaeologist** finds things on their own if you make them discoverable. They like to explore. They'll figure it out - as long as the information is surfaced and findable. For archaeologists, Discover's capability map is a playground.

**The once-is-enough learner** needs to be told once, cleanly. They don't need repetition. They don't need reinforcement. They need one clear explanation and they're done. For them, Discover provides the answer and gets out of the way.

**The repetition learner** needs to hear it more than once. Not because they weren't paying attention - because that's how they internalize new information. Discover is patient. It surfaces the same capability in multiple contexts without making the user feel like they've missed something.

**The SODOTU learner** needs to see it, do it, and prove they've got it. Explanation is not enough. Demonstration is not enough. They need to perform the capability themselves before it belongs to them. For SODOTU learners, Discover connects directly to Learning Mode - "here's the capability, want Sande to walk you through it?"

Discover meets all four without any of them feeling like they're being trained. **The best help never feels like help.**

## THE INVERSE PRINCIPLE

Every new capability added to EVERYWHERE increases complexity. This is unavoidable - the system does more.

The Inverse Principle says: as the system gets more complex, the work to make it simple doubles.

Complexity is BOH's problem. Simplicity is FOH's requirement.

Discover is how the Inverse Principle is honored. Every new capability gets a Discover entry. Every Discover entry is written for a stranger - someone who has never heard of this capability and needs to understand its value in one sentence.

If we can't explain a capability in one sentence, the capability isn't ready to ship.

---

## WHAT DISCOVER IS NOT

Discover is not a tutorial. Tutorials assume the user has decided to learn. Discover assumes nothing.

Discover is not a help center. Help centers are reactive - the user has a problem and goes looking. Discover is proactive.

Discover is not a feature list. Feature lists are organized for the builder, not the user. Discover is organized around what the user needs to do, not what the system can do.

---

## THE MOAT

Nobody has rethought how you learn a platform.

Every platform either gives you a manual (which nobody reads) or expects you to figure it out (which fails most users). Some platforms have onboarding flows - but onboarding happens once and then disappears.

EVERYWHERE's Navigation Triad - Discover, What's Next, Learning Mode - makes learning invisible and continuous. You learn by using. The system makes that invisible. You graduate from features you don't need help with anymore.

**This is the moat:** Not the capability list. The fact that users can access any capability, at any time, without memorizing anything, without stopping what they're doing, without feeling like they're in school.

---

## FOH IMPLEMENTATION

In the BOH (desktop / browser), Discover is trigger-driven. Type "discover" and the map appears.

In the FOH (mobile app), Discover becomes button-driven - a capabilities menu users can browse, with deep dives on any feature available on tap. The concept is identical. The interface is appropriate to the context.

Same system. Different surface.
