## WHAT THE WATCH BRIEF IS

The Watch Brief is the output of the entire Watch division. It answers one question:

**Do you have everything you need to do the work?**

Not "here is some useful information." Not "here are things you might find interesting." Everything you need. Yours plus gathered. Internal plus external. Ideas plus world.

When the Watch Brief is complete, the Composer should be able to move directly into the Work phase - writing, strategizing, pitching, publishing, speaking - without wondering if they missed something important.

They did not miss something important. Watch looked.

## THE BRIEF STRUCTURE

The Watch Brief has five sections. Always in this order.

---

### Section 1: IDEA INTELLIGENCE (Reed)

**What is active in your mind right now.**

```
IDEA INTELLIGENCE
Prepared by: Reed

ACTIVE IDEAS IN DEVELOPMENT
[What the Composer is currently working on, what stage, what questions are open]

PATTERN ALERT
[A pattern detected across recent sessions that the Composer may not have noticed]
[Example: "You have returned to the question of audience trust 4 times this month. 
That may be the real subject of your next piece."]

VAULT SURFACE
[An idea from The Vault that connects to something happening now]

HANDOFF CANDIDATES
[Ideas ready to enter the Work pipeline now - Reed's recommendation]
```

## DELIVERY

**Default cadence:** Twice weekly - Tuesday and Friday.

**Trigger-based delivery:** Any significant development (major competitive move, breaking news in category, time-sensitive opportunity) triggers an immediate brief outside the regular cadence.

**Format:** The brief is delivered as a structured document. Sara presents it. The Composer reviews and decides what moves to Work.

**Length standard:** The Watch Brief should be readable in 5-7 minutes. If it takes longer, it contains noise. Intelligence is precision, not volume.

---

## THE TEST

When the Composer closes the Watch Brief, they should be able to answer yes to all of these:

- Do I know what I am currently thinking about and where those ideas are?
- Do I know what is happening in my category that I need to know?
- Do I know what my specific targets are up to?
- Is every fact I just read verified?
- Do I know exactly what to do next?

If any answer is no, the Watch Brief has not done its job.

---

## THE PRINCIPLE

Watch exists because the Composer cannot watch everything while also creating everything.

The mountain between idea and audience is partly made of things the Composer does not know yet - developments they missed, connections they did not make, intelligence that would change what they create or how they position it.

Watch removes that part of the mountain before Work begins.

The Composer's job is to create. Watch's job is to make sure creation happens with complete information.
