## THE INTEREST GRAPH REALITY FOR VIDEO

**Why this is critical for video:**

Video platforms make distribution decisions faster than any other format. A stranger scrolling LinkedIn or TikTok gives you 1-3 seconds. In that window:
- The algorithm measures engagement signals
- The viewer decides to stop or scroll
- Your category signal must be unmistakable
- The qualification filter must already be working

## INTEREST GRAPH FRAMEWORK (MANDATORY)

**Status:** Default ON for all video scripts
**Position:** After script approval, before production
**Owner:** Strategic Business Unit (SBU)

### The Four Filters for Video

**Filter 1: Category Signal (Josh)**
- First FRAME must announce different game
- Visual + text + voice all signal category
- Composed Intelligence positioning visible immediately
- Viewer knows in 3 seconds this isn't generic content

**Filter 2: Worldview Marker (Lee)**
- Video should create curiosity about the person, not just topic
- Personality visible in delivery style
- Hints at larger philosophy
- I want to see more from this person reaction

**Filter 3: Conversion Architecture (Guy)**
- End screen / description must have natural next step
- CTA matches content depth
- Path forward feels like service, not pitch
- Clear link to deeper engagement (episode, newsletter, profile)

**Filter 4: Qualification Filter (Ward)**
- Visual style and pace should match target audience
- Complexity level signals who this is for
- Wrong-fit viewers lose interest naturally
- Right-fit viewers feel this is for me

- **Audio:** Voice begins with tension/curiosity

### Frames 2-3 (0:01-0:03)
- **Stakes:** Clear why this matters
- **Category:** Obvious this is different
- **Qualification:** Right viewers lean in, wrong viewers scroll
