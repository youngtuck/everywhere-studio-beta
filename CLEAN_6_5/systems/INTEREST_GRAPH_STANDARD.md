## THE SHIFT

**Social Graph Era (2009–2023):** Your feed was determined by who you followed. Content reached people through networks. Building audience meant building follower count.

**Interest Graph Era (2024+):** Algorithms serve content based on behavior, not connections. A piece from an account with 6 followers can outperform a piece from someone with millions. Creative is the variable of success.

**What this means for EVERYWHERE:** Every piece of content may reach strangers who have zero context about the creator. The content must work in isolation AND create a path to relationship.

**Source of term:** Gary Vaynerchuk first articulated the interest graph shift as the foundational change in content distribution. His PAC framework (Platform + Audience + Culture) establishes that understanding each platform's specific content culture is non-negotiable for earned reach. Priya Kumar monitors his current thinking weekly.

## THE FIVE FILTERS

Every piece of content passing through this standard must satisfy five requirements. These are not quality measures - Betterish handles quality. These are business architecture requirements.

### Filter 1: Category Signal (Josh)

**Question:** Does this piece announce a different game, not just a good take?

**What we're looking for:**
- Content signals that a new category exists (Composed Intelligence, not AI tools)
- Viewer/reader recognizes this isn't the same conversation everyone else is having
- The framing itself is distinctive, not just the insight

**Pass criteria:**
- Category language appears naturally, not forced
- Piece couldn't be easily produced by someone in the old category
- Reader thinks "I didn't know this existed" - not just "that's smart"

**Failure pattern:**
- Content that's good but sounds like every other AI/content/business piece
- Insights without distinctive framing
- Could have been written by any thoughtful person in the space

---

### Filter 2: Worldview Marker (Lea)

**Question:** Does this piece make them want to see what else you think?

**What we're looking for:**
- Evidence of a larger intellectual framework beyond this single piece
- Signals that there's more where this came from
- A perspective that creates curiosity about the person, not just the topic

**Pass criteria:**
- Piece contains at least one moment that hints at broader philosophy
- Reader finishes thinking about the creator's worldview, not just the content
- Natural "what else does this person think about X?" reaction

**Failure pattern:**
- Self-contained insight with no thread to pull
- Content that satisfies completely (no reason to seek more)
- Expertise demonstrated but personality absent

---

### Filter 3: Conversion Architecture (Guy)

**Question:** Is there a natural next step built into the format itself?

**What we're looking for:**
- The content structure creates a logical path forward
- Not a bolted-on CTA, but architecture that flows toward deeper engagement
- The "what's next" feels like service, not sales

**Pass criteria:**
- Clear next step exists and feels natural
- Path forward is specific (not "follow me for more")
- Conversion mechanism matches content depth (light content → light ask; deep content → deeper invitation)

**Appropriate next steps by format:**
- **Sunday Story:** Subscribe, explore related thinking, share with someone specific
- **Get Current:** Episode subscription, topic deep-dive, community access
- **LinkedIn:** Comment engagement, profile visit, newsletter signup
- **Video:** Full episode, related content, direct connection

**Failure pattern:**
- Great content with no path forward
- Generic "follow for more" that doesn't match the content's specificity
- CTA that feels disconnected from what they just consumed

---

### Filter 4: Qualification Filter (Ward)

**Question:** Does this content attract the right people and repel the wrong ones?

**What we're looking for:**
- Content contains signals that self-select the audience
- Wrong-fit prospects lose interest naturally
- Right-fit prospects feel "this is for me"

**Pass criteria:**
- Piece takes a clear stance - not "it depends"
- Complexity level matches target audience; doesn't dumb down for mass appeal
- Values are visible (martial arts tenets, collaborative philosophy, etc.)

**Qualification signals to embed:**
- Assumption that the reader thinks for themselves
- References to operator experience, not theoretical knowledge
- Respect for craft and rigor
- Impatience with hype and shortcuts

**Failure pattern:**
- Content optimized for maximum reach instead of right reach
- Watered-down positions to avoid alienating anyone
- Mass-appeal framing that attracts tire-kickers

---

### Filter 5: Platform Nativity (Jordan Lane - evaluated at WRAP)

**Question:** Does this content feel like it was made for this specific channel - or like it was poured in from somewhere else?

**What we're looking for:**
- Format conventions of the platform are respected and used
- Content uses the platform's vocabulary and register
- Length, pacing, and structure match what that platform's audience expects

**Platform-native signals:**
- LinkedIn: white space, short paragraphs, punchy opening line, no hashtag stuffing
- YouTube: chapter markers, retention arc, visual cue language
- Substack: editorial voice, longer paragraphs acceptable, personal address
- TikTok/Reels: hook in first 2 seconds, vertical-native framing, pattern interrupts
- Podcast: conversational scaffolding, natural transitions, no list-reading

**When this filter runs:** During WRAP, not during production. Jordan Lane owns this check at Checkpoint 2 when a specific channel is assigned.

**Failure pattern:**
- Essay pasted verbatim into LinkedIn with no reformatting
- Podcast script read like a document
- TikTok copy that sounds like a blog post

## PIPELINE POSITION

```
Content Creation (Reed + Team)
        ↓
Quality Checkpoints (Betterish + Editorial - Checkpoints 0-6)
        ↓
Interest Graph Standard (SBU) ← Filters 1–4
        ↓
Platform Nativity (Jordan Lane at WRAP) ← Filter 5
        ↓
Format Optimization (Dmitri)
        ↓
Publication
```

**Relationship to Betterish:** Betterish asks "Is this good enough to publish?" The Interest Graph Standard asks "Is this engineered for algorithmic discovery and business conversion?" A piece can score 850 Betterish (Spicy Genius) and still fail this standard. Both must pass.

**Relationship to Quality Checkpoints:** This standard runs AFTER editorial quality checkpoints pass. We don't optimize for algorithms until we've confirmed the content meets publication standards.

## FAILURE RECOVERY

**Single filter failure:** Specific revision guidance provided. Content returns to relevant team member for targeted fix. Re-review on failed filter only.

**Multiple filter failure:** SBU provides comprehensive revision brief. Content may need structural rework, not just polish. Reed or originating team member leads revision. Full re-review required.

**Persistent failure (two rounds):** Escalate to Mark. Options: significant rework, repurpose for different channel, or accept as documented exception.

## SUCCESS METRICS

**Leading indicators (monthly):**
- % of content passing standard on first review
- Average revision cycles before pass
- Filter-specific pass rates (identify weak spots)

**Lagging indicators (quarterly):**
- Engagement from non-followers (Interest Graph reach)
- Profile visits/follows from content (Worldview pull)
- Conversion rates by content piece (Architecture effectiveness)
- Lead quality from content-originated prospects (Qualification accuracy)
